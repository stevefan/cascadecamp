Small notes for finding your way.

A public-facing guide for arrival, meals, sessions, site norms, and the shared practices that help a participant-made camp feel held.

Start Here

How to use this.

Cascade Camp is a stone-soup gathering. The organizers hold the pot: place, meals, basic rhythm, safety practices, and enough structure for people to find each other.

The contents are made by the people who come: sessions, tea, doodles, walks, campfire songs, small rituals, conversations, quiet time, games, and practical help around camp.

  • Read the packing notes before you leave.
  • Find the meal board and session board when you arrive.
  • Ask before photographing or recording people.
  • Notice what needs doing and pitch in lightly.
  • Bring your offering, even if it is small.

Rhythm

Tentative meal and ceremony anchors.

The exact schedule is still TBD. Most sessions will be arranged at camp on a whiteboard after breakfast, but these anchors are expected:

Friday Arrival window, dinner around 7pm, opening circle.
Saturday Breakfast, ad hoc session board, lunch, dinner, night texture.
Sunday Breakfast, sessions, lunch, dinner, closing circle.
Monday Breakfast, packing, goodbyes, leave the site kinder than we found it.

Packing

Bring the boring magic.

  • Tent, stakes, rainfly, ground cloth
  • Sleeping bag, pad, pillow, warm layers
  • Headlamp or flashlight
  • Camping chair or sit pad
  • Towel and bathing suit for sauna or creek
  • Comfortable shoes for uneven ground
  • Medications, toiletries, sunscreen
  • Battery pack and charging cable
  • Snacks or drinks that make you happy
  • Your session, craft, game, walk, song, or question

Site Norms

How we keep it held.

Lanyards

Lanyards help communicate recording comfort. Treat them as a starting point, then ask before making someone the subject of a photo or video.

Bathrooms

The site uses composting toilets. They are nicer than porta-potties, but they work differently. Read the posted instructions and ask if unsure.

Fire, Sauna, Water

Use ordinary outdoor care around fire, sauna, creek, trails, tools, and site infrastructure. Do not make risk someone else's problem.

Camp Jobs

Expect light shared work: dishes, breakfast setup, tidying common spaces, noticing what needs doing, and helping without drama.
